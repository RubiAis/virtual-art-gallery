✧ ART HISTORY TIMELINE ✧

1400s — Renaissance

A cultural rebirth that celebrated classical art, architecture, and the beauty of the human form. Artists like Botticelli and Michelangelo elevated artistic expression through harmony, anatomy, and perspective.

1700s — Romanticism

An emotional and imaginative movement focused on nature, beauty, and individual expression. Artists sought to capture deep feelings, heroic themes, and dramatic moments in time.

1800s — Impressionism

A break from tradition using light, movement, and color to capture moments in time. Painters like Monet and Degas used quick brushstrokes to reflect modern life and fleeting beauty.

1900s — Surrealism

A dreamlike movement that explored the subconscious through strange, symbolic imagery. Surrealists like Dalí and Magritte challenged reality through fantastical compositions.

1300s – Gothic Art

A style that flourished in medieval Europe, characterized by religious themes, elongated figures, and dramatic expressions. Artists like Giotto paved the way for the Renaissance.

1500s – Mannerism

A reaction to the harmony of the Renaissance, Mannerism featured exaggerated poses, unusual scale, and bold colors. Artists like El Greco created emotionally intense works.

1600s – Baroque

Known for its drama, movement, and grandeur. Baroque art often focused on intense light and shadow, with artists like Caravaggio and Rembrandt leading the way.

1700s – Neoclassicism

Inspired by ancient Greece and Rome, this movement emphasized order, logic, and heroic themes. Artists like Jacques-Louis David created works full of moral seriousness.

1950s – Abstract Expressionism

Centered in New York, this post-war movement focused on spontaneous, expressive brushwork. Jackson Pollock and Mark Rothko are iconic figures of this abstract style.

1960s – Pop Art

A fun and colorful movement that drew inspiration from popular culture, advertising, and comics. Andy Warhol and Roy Lichtenstein made everyday objects into high art.
